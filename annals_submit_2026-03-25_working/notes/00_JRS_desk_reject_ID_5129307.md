## Email text

Dear Dr. Ryota Matsuki,

Thank you for submitting your manuscript, "Public Co-Creation Hubs, Cross-Regional Pipelines, and Dynamic Efficiency in Thin Regional Ecosystems," to the Journal of Regional Science.

As part of our editorial process, submissions are evaluated by the editors to assess their fit with the journal's readership and standards. Given the selectivity of the journal and the volume of submissions we receive, we must make difficult decisions at an early stage.

After careful consideration, we have decided not to move forward with the manuscript. The paper addresses a relevant regional-policy question and is clearly written, but in our view the contribution remains too stylized and assumption-driven to provide a sufficiently compelling theoretical advance for the journal. The central results follow quite directly from the reduced-form structure of the model, particularly the imposed novelty-loss mechanism and the assumption that pipeline policy generates value only in conjunction with a hub. As a result, the conclusion that bundled hub-plus-pipeline policy can dominate a stand-alone hub appears built into the setup more than derived from a richer model of regional interaction.

What weakens it most is that the case for public intervention is not derived from a decentralized equilibrium or a clearly modeled distortion, but from an ad hoc short-run "private evaluation" rule. Region B is also largely passive, and there is no endogenous network formation, migration, location choice, calibration, or empirical validation. The empirical predictions are sensible, but they remain suggestive rather than probative.

For these reasons, we do not believe the manuscript meets the level of rigor and contribution we would require for publication in JRS. We appreciate the opportunity to consider your work and hope these comments prove useful as you revise the paper for another outlet.

We thank you for considering the journal and, despite this disappointing outcome, we hope you will continue to view the Journal of Regional Science as a potential outlet for your future research.

Sincerely,

Luisa Corrado  
Co-Editor  
Journal of Regional Science
